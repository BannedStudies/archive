If you would like to submit a syllabus, we ask that you please fill out [this interest form](https://forms.gle/p4Nb3E5ivL5Mo4z18).

If you would like to contact the Banned Studies Archive team, please contact us directly at bannedstudies at proton dot me .

## Submission Process

After you submit a response to the interest form, or contact the Archive directly, we will reach out to you directly over your preferred contact method.

When we contact you, we will ask some information about you, your syllabi, and the challenges you have experienced. Because these conversations can involve sensitive information and sensitive subjects, we will first ask if there is a preferred, secure messaging platform.

As we talk with you, we will ask about a syllabus (or syllabi) you are hoping to donate. The donation process will involve sending the document over email (or other preferred communication) as both a .pdf file and as a text-editable file (Word Document, markdown document, plain text document). We will also need some additional information during this submission:


    Course Title:
    School/Institution:
    Course Level:
    School Department:
    Academic Discipline(s): (Keywords)
    Reason the Class was Cancelled: (This can also include information if your role was terminated)
    Year the Syllabus Covers:
    Term this Syllabus Covers:
    Short Intro: Short blurb for the syllabus card (30 Words Max)
    Long Intro: An abstract summarizing the course, and learning objectives (Up to 500 Words)


## Anonymity and Consent

During submission, we will have you fill out a short form asking for your consent to retain and/or publish submitted files. **If at any time you decide you want to remove your submission, you can revoke consent and we will delete the files from the online site as well as in our offline collections.**

We are choosing to have two archives--an online and an offline one--modelling the approach to digital archiving established by [the Mukurtu team](https://mukurtu.org/). Our hope is to create an archive of banned syllabi to make the content of those courses available to everyone who wishes to access them, but we also acknowledge that openly publishing this information could be used by bad faith actors. We also acknowledge that our use of GitHub Pages to host this site, means that the data included is easily accessible for web crawlers, scraping data for Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI), especially Microsoft's CoPilot. We are creating an offline source to preserve syllabi from educators who may face retaliation (internally or externally), and who still wish to preserve their work.

Because of our concerns about donator safety, we have decided to remove all personal identifying information from all syllabi submitted (both online and offline). This will include (but is not limited to) names, emails, office location, and phone numbers. We plan to include information about your institution, but we can work with you should you need additional information removed from the syllabi for your safety.

When you submit a syllabi, we will ask for the following:

1. Your affirmative consent to submit the syllabus/syllabi.
2. Your choice to submit it for the online or offline archive. (Online archive submissions will be backed up in the offline archive automatically.)
3. Any specific requests for anonymity or protections.

## Final Notes

We are hoping to make the submission process a point of contact and collaboration. We have set protocols for making the documents go online, but we want to prioritize the needs of our donators. This means that we are happy to be flexible to your needs, your worries, and your desires.
